Lesson 1

Signs of IVDD


Back or Neck Pain: When a dog is in severe pain, however, the tummy can feel hard and rigid. That rigidness is a defense mechanism, a way for the dog to try and stop any further movement that could trigger more pain.

Arching Back: An injury to the upper back/neck will cause pain and possibly stiffness. A dog might arch his/her back as a result of pain or injury due to a herniated disc.

Weakeness: If your dog can walk but is weak or wobbly in the rear legs, it could mean that extruded material from a slipped disc is pressing on the spinal cord. with the fibrous tissues underneath.

Lesson 2

Critical Care


Incision Area: Monitor the incision are every day, It should be clean and dry all the time.

Incontinence: Look after bladder distarge, keep the urinary area always clean and dry, using dog shampoo or bottom proof bedding.

Home Therapy: It reduces any swelling or spasms.

Walkingyour Pet: Walk your dog 3 to 4 time a day, using a walking sling, by lifting the hips and keeping dog paws on the ground while walking, by correcting the standing position.

Lesson 3

Post-Surgical Therapy


Deep Tissue Massage: 15 minutes daily.

Passive Range of Motion: 10-15 repetitions on each affected limb, 1-2 times daily.

Withdrawal Reflex: 5 repetitions on each affected limb, 1-2 times daily.

Cookies Streches: 10 repetitions for each direction, 1-2 times daily.

Weight Shifting: 10-20 repetitions for each direction,1-2 times daily.
